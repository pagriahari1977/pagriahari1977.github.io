    Adventure-Based MMORPG

    For fans that enjoyed playing Old School Runescape on PC back in the day, the revival of this game in 2015 must have brought back nostalgic memories. But, this game’s revival comes as no surprise. It is arguably one of the most popular MMORPG. Runescape has a large following, so much so that the Jagex Games Studio created a mobile version as well. Whether you prefer to play OSRS on the go or in vintage fashion on your computer, there is no denying that this game offers a great sense of freewheeling adventure.

    A Vast Open World to Explore

    With no main storyline, Runescape lets players define their own adventures. Your character will traverse a vast open world. The game is set in the mythical medieval fantasy world of Gelinor. Players use a mini-map to guide their character through different towns, cities, kingdoms and Islands. From the Tutorial Island, the game drops you in the hometown of Lumbridge. From here, you begin different lore-based quests. Successful completion of the task at hand will earn you quest points. But, to take part in these quests, first, you must acquire skills. These are player attributes that involve fishing, trading, cutting wood, fire making, mining, slaying monsters, among other activities.

    Interactive Gameplay

    When playing Old School Runescape on PC or mobile, you will interact a lot with non-player characters (NPCs). You cannot attack NPCs. However, players can hold dialogues with these computer controlled characters. From bankers to store owners to civilians, there are all kinds of NPCs in the game. To identify these special characters, you will see their names highlighted in yellow on the mini-map. They perform predefined functions like giving out information on quests or selling items. Other NPCs are just there for conversation. They help to bring out the British humor that OSRS is known for and set the game’s witty atmosphere.

    The Old School Runescape wiki offers a wealth of information on how to play this game. It has plenty of quest guides for novice players. However, the wiki guides also come in handy if you find yourself stuck in a difficult quest.

    • Invest in Essential Supplies
    Some quests reward you with OSRS gold coins upon successful completion. Once you make enough money, you can start to trade and buy items from Runescape’s auction house, the Grand Exchange. Make sure to buy the most basic supplies. A few good examples include food to recover your horsepower/health, magic runes for casting spells, body armor for protection, and portions to boost your skills.
